        <li><strong>Game Theory Primer:</strong> Framework for anticipating state actor behavior based on incentive structures, not threat intelligence speculation</li>

        <li><strong>Implementation Timeline Forecasting:</strong> Convert policy signals into technical roadmaps with resource allocation guidance</li>

        <h2>Incentive Analysis for Control Design</h2>
    </div>

The underlying thesis: incentive structures predict compliance outcomes more reliably than audit findings or tool selections.
    </p>

    <h3>Core Components</h3>
    <ul>
        <li><strong>Stakeholder Incentive Mapping:</strong> Systematic analysis of auditor, engineer, and executive motivations that shape control implementation and assessment outcomes</li>
        <li><strong>Compliance-in-Operations Architecture:</strong> Design patterns that embed compliance verification into engineering workflows rather than bolting on audit theater</li>
        <li><strong>Behavioral Prediction Models:</strong> Framework for forecasting control adoption patterns based on stakeholder incentives, not policy mandates</li>
    </ul>
